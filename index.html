Phase 0B — Briefing

Program Briefing Script

Read this aloud to Kaplan before Q1. Approximately 1 minute. Slow, deliberate delivery — this sets the tone of the entire interview.

YOU
"Hello, I'm Yang Se-hee, producer of YTN's [The Prompt]."
안녕하세요. YTN [더 프롬프트]의 제작진 양세희 피디입니다.
YOU
"A new wave has begun. Many say the moment when artificial intelligence surpasses human intelligence — the 'singularity' — is almost here. But what will that future actually look like?"
AI라는 새로운 물결이 시작됐고, 인공지능의 지능과 기술이 인간을 넘어서는 시기, 즉 '특이점'이 곧 온다고 하는데 과연 그 미래는 어떨까.
YOU
"Who will benefit from the social and economic changes of the AI era? If the economy grows, will that wealth be distributed fairly? Will my job really survive? What kind of future will my children face? We are living through an age full of question marks."
AI 시대에 사회적, 경제적인 새로운 변화들은 누구를 위한 것인가? 경제적 성장이 이루어진다면, 부의 분배는 공평할까? 나의 직업은 정말 사라지지 않을지, 나의 자녀는 어떤 미래를 맞게 될지, 물음표가 가득한 시대를 통과 중입니다.
YOU
"That's why YTN created [The Prompt] — to ask how we should live in the age of AI, and to seek answers about humanity's future."
그래서 YTN은 [더 프롬프트]를 통해 AI 시대에 어떻게 살아야 하는지, 인류의 미래를 묻고자 합니다.
YOU
"The word 'Prompt' here is not just a command we give to AI. We know the difference between an answer that simply retrieves information, and a real answer that includes process and future."
여기서 '프롬프트'는 AI에게 답을 요구하는 명령어가 아닙니다. '정보 취합의 답'과 '과정과 미래를 포함한 해답'의 차이를 우리는 알고 있습니다.
YOU
"That is why we are meeting with leading thinkers from around the world — to find human answers to the question of how we should face this new era of revolution."
저희는 전 세계 글로벌 지성들을 직접 만나 '새로운 혁명의 시기'를 어떻게 마주해야 할지 '인간의 관점이 담긴 현답'을 찾아보려고 합니다.
YOU
"It is truly an honor to have you with us today."
모시게 되어, 영광입니다.
Delivery Note

Pause slightly before the final line. "모시게 되어, 영광입니다" / "It is truly an honor" — this lands best with a beat of silence before it. Make eye contact. Don't rush.

교수님, 가장 기본적인 질문부터 시작할게요. 당신은 AI 시대를 환영하는가?

Q1

Do you welcome the age of AI?

당신은 AI 시대를 환영하는가?
▼
Expected Answer

He'll say yes — but immediately reframe. "Of course. But let's be clear: we're welcoming a powerful new tool, not a new form of intelligence." He sees AI's impact like the invention of the wheel — world-changing but not apocalyptic. Star Trek, not Terminator.

"You've said before that AI is closer to Star Trek than Terminator — is that still how you'd put it?"
전에 AI가 터미네이터보다는 스타트렉에 가깝다고 하셨는데 — 지금도 같은 생각이신가요?

Q1–1

You call it a tool — do you actually use them yourself? Claude, Gemini? How much do you trust them?

도구라고 하셨는데 — 본인은 직접 쓰시나요? 얼마나 신뢰하시나요?
▼
Expected Answer

He uses generative AI and has learned from it. But he's cautious on trust — AI can recite rules but cannot execute them. It doesn't understand, it outputs. Useful, not blindly trustworthy.

If he doesn't mention specific tools

Section 2 › Will AI Bring a New Golden Age? 5 min
Section 2 — Reframing AI

Will AI Bring a
New Golden Age?

This section challenges popular AGI/superintelligence narratives. Let Kaplan correct the record on what AI actually is. Then connect to the human cost of transition.

Q2

People talk about AI as if it thinks, reasons, even feels. Is AI truly unprecedented — or just the latest chapter in automation history?

많은 사람들이 AI를 마치 생각하고, 추론하고, 심지어 감정까지 느끼는 존재처럼 이야기합니다. AI는 정말 인류가 한 번도 경험하지 못한 새로운 무언가인가요?
▼
Expected Answer

"The original sin is the name itself." 'Artificial Intelligence' implies machines think like us — they don't. Deep learning, neural networks — analogies, not descriptions. On Bostrom/superintelligence: "Bostrom is a great guy, but bad idea." Machines have no independent desires. AI = latest chapter in automation. Nothing more.

Key phrases to listen for

"Machines can perform tasks humans solve using intelligence — but that doesn't mean machines are intelligent." / "Neural networks is little more than an analogy."

If he doesn't address AGI specifically

"You've literally said 'Bostrom is a great guy, but bad idea' — what's the flaw in his superintelligence argument?"
"보스트롬은 좋은 사람이지만 나쁜 생각이다"라고 하셨잖아요 — 그의 초지능 논리의 어디가 잘못된 건가요?

Q2–1

But AI is moving so much faster than any previous automation. Work, identity, quality of life — everything is shifting at once. Will people actually have time to adapt?

하지만 AI는 그보다 훨씬 빠르게 움직이고 있습니다. 노동도, 정체성도, 삶의 질까지 모든 게 동시에 흔들리는 상황에서 우리에게 과연 적응할 시간이 주어지긴 할까요?
▼
Expected Answer

Optimistic but realistic. 200 years ago, 90% of Americans worked in agriculture — now 2%, yet everyone found new work. Automation always creates opportunity eventually. The problem is the word "eventually." People get hurt in transitions. Solution: redesign job training and social safety nets — now, not later.

If he's too optimistic / glosses over human cost

"But 'eventually' is a long time when you're the one who lost your job at 50. What do we owe those people in the meantime?"
하지만 50살에 직업을 잃은 사람에게 '언젠가는'은 너무 긴 시간이잖아요. 그 사람들에게 우리는 무엇을 해줘야 할까요?

Q2–2 Bridge

So — will AI actually bring us a new golden age? A new Renaissance?

그렇다면 결국 — 우리는 르네상스나 벨에포크처럼 또 한 번의 황금기를 맞게 될까요?
▼
Expected Answer (Bridge)

Short: "It can — but it won't happen on its own." This naturally leads into labor & distribution.

Pivot note

This is a deliberate bridge question. Don't let him expand too far — redirect to Section 3 as soon as he finishes the thought.

→ Transition into Section 3

"Right — so the golden age doesn't come automatically. Which brings us to the question of who benefits, and who gets left behind. Let's talk about labor."
맞아요 — 황금기는 자동으로 오지 않죠. 그러면 누가 혜택을 받고, 누가 소외되는지 얘기해봐야 할 것 같아요. 노동 이야기를 해볼게요.

Section 3 › The Future of Labor & Wealth Distribution 8 min
Section 3 — Core Economic Territory

The Future of Labor
& Wealth Distribution

This is the meat of the interview. Kaplan's most original contributions are here — tasks vs. jobs, capital accumulation, progressive tax proposals. Give this room to breathe.

Important book context

Humans Need Not Apply (2015) was widely discussed in Korea — mention this when you get to Q3-1. It flatters him and localizes the conversation for your Korean audience.

Q3

Many people assume creative jobs are safe and blue-collar jobs are most at risk. Is that actually how AI disrupts work — or are we thinking about this completely wrong?

많은 사람들이 창의적인 직업은 안전하고 블루칼라 직업이 가장 위험하다고 생각합니다. 우리가 완전히 잘못된 프레임으로 보고 있는 건가요?
▼
Expected Answer — Key Insight

"Completely wrong frame." AI automates tasks, not jobs. A doctor's routine lab reviews can be automated — not their empathy or complex judgment. A plumber in unpredictable physical environments is harder to automate than a lawyer writing standard contracts. A 3-year-old can do things machines have no idea how to replicate. Future luxury industry = entirely human-to-human service (budget hotel: machine check-in; high-end hotel: human staff).

If he's abstract — ask for examples

"Could you give us a specific example — maybe a profession people think is safe that's actually very exposed, or vice versa?"
구체적인 예를 들어주실 수 있나요? 안전하다고 생각하지만 실제로는 위험한 직업, 혹은 그 반대의 경우요?

Q3–1

You wrote Humans Need Not Apply ten years ago — a book that was widely read in Korea too. What did you get right, and what did you get wrong?

10년 전 '인간은 필요 없다'를 쓰셨는데, 한국에서도 많이 화제가 됐던 책이에요. 맞은 것과 틀린 것은 무엇인가요?
▼
Expected Answer

Direction was right. AI disrupting labor markets — correct. But white-collar professionals (lawyers, journalists, doctors) being hit this fast was faster than he expected. Generative AI in particular exceeded his predictions.

Q3–2

But work isn't just about money — it gives people identity and purpose. If AI takes that away, what fills the void?

하지만 노동은 단순히 돈만이 아닙니다 — 정체성과 삶의 목적을 줍니다. AI가 그것을 빼앗아 간다면, 무엇이 그 빈자리를 채울 수 있을까요?
▼
Expected Answer

In the 19th century people worked 60–80 hours/week. Today half that, and we're far richer. Our great-grandchildren may see our concept of "real work" as a quaint 21st century idea. New meaningful activities always emerge — competitive gamers, artisans, flower arrangers, party planners. Human desires expand with wealth.

Q3–3

If AI grows the economy — can we actually build a future where that wealth is shared fairly?

AI가 경제를 성장시킨다면, 부의 분배 문제는 어떻게 하나요? 공평한 미래를 만들 순 없을까요?
▼
Expected Answer — Policy Proposal

Possible but not automatic. Automation benefits naturally flow to capital owners — Karl Marx predicted this and reality is proving it. His proposed solution: progressive corporate tax. Companies with broader shareholder bases pay lower taxes. Goal: not redistribute wealth, but design structures that create broader ownership from the start. The free market mythology must be abandoned.

If he mentions Marx

"That's an interesting invocation of Marx from someone working in Silicon Valley — how do you think about that tension?"
실리콘밸리에서 일하시는 분이 마르크스를 언급하시는 게 흥미로운데요 — 그 긴장감을 어떻게 생각하세요?

Q3–4

So what needs to change first — education or policy?

그렇다면 지금 당장 가장 시급하게 바꿔야 할 것은 무엇인가요? 교육인가요, 제도인가요?
▼
Expected Answer

Vocational training is "really messed up." Government acts as primary lender for student loans, disconnecting education from market needs. His idea: a "job mortgage" — borrow against future earning potential to learn marketable skills. Real investment in education, not just handing out money for unemployable degrees.

→ Transition into Section 4

"But all of that — the distribution, the policy, the golden age — it's all built on the assumption that the AI economy keeps growing. So let's talk about that assumption. Because you've lived through four AI bubbles. Is this one different?"
그런데 분배도, 제도도, 황금기도 — 결국 AI 경제가 계속 성장한다는 전제 위에 있잖아요. 그 전제 자체를 한번 따져보고 싶어요. 교수님은 AI 버블을 네 번이나 겪으셨으니까요. 이번엔 다를까요?

Section 4 › AI Bubble & The Limits of LLMs 7 min
Section 4 — Financial Reality Check

AI Bubble &
The Limits of LLMs

Kaplan has lived through multiple AI winters. He's one of the few people who can talk about the bubble with genuine authority — and he's worried about individual investors specifically.

Q4

Reference › 5 Core Positions
Quick Reference

Kaplan's 5 Core
Positions

Read these before the interview. If his answers seem to contradict these — that's a follow-up moment.

1 — AI = Automation, Not Intelligence

AI is not a new form of intelligence — it is the latest chapter in a long history of automation. The name 'Artificial Intelligence' was coined by John McCarthy largely for marketing purposes and has misled the public ever since.

2 — Machines Don't Think — They Simulate

Current AI systems don't reason — they simulate reasoning. They produce the most statistically plausible next output based on training data. That's the difference between real and simulated reasoning.

3 — AI Bubble Will Burst

This is his fourth AI bubble in a lifetime. The scale is 6× bigger than the dot-com crash, and this time individual investors are being pulled in through opaque financial instruments. The technology is real — the investment scale is a bubble.

4 — Jobs vs. Tasks

AI doesn't replace jobs — it automates tasks within jobs. Stop asking 'which professions are safe?' and start asking 'which tasks are automatable?' Even doctors have automatable tasks. Even 3-year-olds can do things machines cannot.

5 — Wealth Distribution is the Real Problem

AI will make society richer — but the benefits naturally flow to those with capital. Karl Marx was right about that. Without intentional policy design (e.g., progressive corporate tax), inequality will worsen. The free market will not fix this on its own.
