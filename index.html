Phase 0B — Briefing

Program Briefing Script

Read this aloud to Kaplan before Q1. Approximately 1 minute. Slow, deliberate delivery — this sets the tone of the entire interview.

YOU
"Hello, I'm Yang Se-hee, producer of YTN's [The Prompt]."
안녕하세요. YTN [더 프롬프트]의 제작진 양세희 피디입니다.
YOU
"A new wave has begun. Many say the moment when artificial intelligence surpasses human intelligence — the 'singularity' — is almost here. But what will that future actually look like?"
AI라는 새로운 물결이 시작됐고, 인공지능의 지능과 기술이 인간을 넘어서는 시기, 즉 '특이점'이 곧 온다고 하는데 과연 그 미래는 어떨까.
YOU
"Who will benefit from the social and economic changes of the AI era? If the economy grows, will that wealth be distributed fairly? Will my job really survive? What kind of future will my children face? We are living through an age full of question marks."
AI 시대에 사회적, 경제적인 새로운 변화들은 누구를 위한 것인가? 경제적 성장이 이루어진다면, 부의 분배는 공평할까? 나의 직업은 정말 사라지지 않을지, 나의 자녀는 어떤 미래를 맞게 될지, 물음표가 가득한 시대를 통과 중입니다.
YOU
"That's why YTN created [The Prompt] — to ask how we should live in the age of AI, and to seek answers about humanity's future."
그래서 YTN은 [더 프롬프트]를 통해 AI 시대에 어떻게 살아야 하는지, 인류의 미래를 묻고자 합니다.
YOU
"The word 'Prompt' here is not just a command we give to AI. We know the difference between an answer that simply retrieves information, and a real answer that includes process and future."
여기서 '프롬프트'는 AI에게 답을 요구하는 명령어가 아닙니다. '정보 취합의 답'과 '과정과 미래를 포함한 해답'의 차이를 우리는 알고 있습니다.
YOU
"That is why we are meeting with leading thinkers from around the world — to find human answers to the question of how we should face this new era of revolution."
저희는 전 세계 글로벌 지성들을 직접 만나 '새로운 혁명의 시기'를 어떻게 마주해야 할지 '인간의 관점이 담긴 현답'을 찾아보려고 합니다.
YOU
"It is truly an honor to have you with us today."
모시게 되어, 영광입니다.
Delivery Note

Pause slightly before the final line. "모시게 되어, 영광입니다" / "It is truly an honor" — this lands best with a beat of silence before it. Make eye contact. Don't rush.

Section 2 › Will AI Bring a New Golden Age? 5 min
Section 2 — Reframing AI

Will AI Bring a
New Golden Age?

This section challenges popular AGI/superintelligence narratives. Let Kaplan correct the record on what AI actually is. Then connect to the human cost of transition.

Q2

People talk about AI as if it thinks, reasons, even feels. Is AI truly unprecedented — or just the latest chapter in automation history?

많은 사람들이 AI를 마치 생각하고, 추론하고, 심지어 감정까지 느끼는 존재처럼 이야기합니다. AI는 정말 인류가 한 번도 경험하지 못한 새로운 무언가인가요?
▼
Expected Answer

"The original sin is the name itself — 'Artificial Intelligence' implies machines think like us, and they don't." He'll call AGI/superintelligence fears a fundamental misunderstanding: "Bostrom is a great guy, but bad idea." AI is simply the latest chapter in a long history of automation.

Key phrases to listen for

"Machines can perform tasks humans solve using intelligence — but that doesn't mean machines are intelligent." / "Neural networks is little more than an analogy."

If he doesn't address AGI specifically

"You've literally said 'Bostrom is a great guy, but bad idea' — what's the flaw in his superintelligence argument?"
"보스트롬은 좋은 사람이지만 나쁜 생각이다"라고 하셨잖아요 — 그의 초지능 논리의 어디가 잘못된 건가요?

Q2–1

But AI is moving so much faster than any previous automation. Work, identity, quality of life — everything is shifting at once. Will people actually have time to adapt?

하지만 AI는 그보다 훨씬 빠르게 움직이고 있습니다. 노동도, 정체성도, 삶의 질까지 모든 게 동시에 흔들리는 상황에서 우리에게 과연 적응할 시간이 주어지긴 할까요?
▼
Expected Answer

He's optimistic but realistic: 200 years ago, 90% of Americans worked in agriculture — now it's 2%, yet everyone found new work. Automation always creates new opportunities, but the problem is the word "eventually" — people get hurt during the transition.

If he's too optimistic / glosses over human cost

"But 'eventually' is a long time when you're the one who lost your job at 50. What do we owe those people in the meantime?"
하지만 50살에 직업을 잃은 사람에게 '언젠가는'은 너무 긴 시간이잖아요. 그 사람들에게 우리는 무엇을 해줘야 할까요?

추가 팔로업 — simulated reasoning으로 파고들기

"You've said machines 'simulate reasoning' rather than actually reason. But to most people watching, the output looks identical. Why does that distinction matter?"
기계가 실제로 추론하는 게 아니라 추론을 '시뮬레이션'한다고 하셨는데, 시청자들 눈에는 결과물이 똑같아 보여요. 왜 그 구분이 중요한가요?

연결 key phrases: "Machines can perform tasks humans solve using intelligence — but that doesn't mean machines are intelligent." / "Neural networks is little more than an analogy — like saying airplane design was inspired by birds." / "The original sin of AI is the name itself."

Q2–2 Bridge

So — will AI actually bring us a new golden age? A new Renaissance?

그렇다면 결국 — 우리는 르네상스나 벨에포크처럼 또 한 번의 황금기를 맞게 될까요?
▼
Expected Answer (Bridge)

Short bridge answer: "It can — but it won't happen on its own." This sets up the labor and wealth distribution discussion in Section 3.

Pivot note

This is a deliberate bridge question. Don't let him expand too far — redirect to Section 3 as soon as he finishes the thought.

→ Transition into Section 3

"Right — so the golden age doesn't come automatically. Which brings us to the question of who benefits, and who gets left behind. Let's talk about labor."
맞아요 — 황금기는 자동으로 오지 않죠. 그러면 누가 혜택을 받고, 누가 소외되는지 얘기해봐야 할 것 같아요. 노동 이야기를 해볼게요.

Section 3 — Core Economic Territory

The Future of Labor
& Wealth Distribution

This is the meat of the interview. Kaplan's most original contributions are here — tasks vs. jobs, capital accumulation, progressive tax proposals. Give this room to breathe.

Important book context

Humans Need Not Apply (2015) was widely discussed in Korea — mention this when you get to Q3-1. It flatters him and localizes the conversation for your Korean audience.

5 Core Positions — 인터뷰 전 필독
1 — AI = Automation, Not Intelligence

AI is not a new form of intelligence — it is the latest chapter in a long history of automation. The name 'Artificial Intelligence' was coined by John McCarthy largely for marketing purposes and has misled the public ever since.

2 — Machines Don't Think — They Simulate

Current AI systems don't reason — they simulate reasoning. They produce the most statistically plausible next output based on training data. That's the difference between real and simulated reasoning.

3 — AI Bubble Will Burst

This is his fourth AI bubble in a lifetime. The scale is 6× bigger than the dot-com crash, and this time individual investors are being pulled in through opaque financial instruments. The technology is real — the investment scale is a bubble.

4 — Jobs vs. Tasks

AI doesn't replace jobs — it automates tasks within jobs. Stop asking 'which professions are safe?' and start asking 'which tasks are automatable?' Even doctors have automatable tasks. Even 3-year-olds can do things machines cannot.

5 — Wealth Distribution is the Real Problem

AI will make society richer — but the benefits naturally flow to those with capital. Karl Marx was right about that. Without intentional policy design (e.g., progressive corporate tax), inequality will worsen. The free market will not fix this on its own.

사람은 존중, 아이디어는 정면 비판. → "What specifically is wrong with the idea?"
패턴 4 "The keyword is 'eventually.'"
낙관론 + 현실의 고통. → "So who helps the people who can't wait for 'eventually'?"
패턴 5 역사 사례 → 현재 적용
농업 90→2%, 1800년 소득 등 항상 역사로 시작. → "And you think the same pattern holds this time — even with the speed AI is moving?"
패턴 6 "A three-year-old / a bear / a chimp can do what machines cannot."
기계의 한계를 구체적으로. → "Is there a category of human ability that you think is permanently out of reach for machines?"
유머 패턴 자기비하 + 과장된 비유
"pontificating pundits — also including myself" 같은 자기비하, "Super Attractiveness" 같은 과장 비유. → 같이 웃고 "That's a funny way to put it — but there's a serious point underneath..." 로 당겨와.
AI 토론 수준 올리는 핵심 용어
"simulated reasoning" vs "actual reasoning"
쓰는 법: "So when we talk about LLMs — are we talking about reasoning, or simulated reasoning?"
"automate tasks, not replace jobs"
쓰는 법: "So the right question isn't 'which jobs are safe' — it's 'which tasks within each job are automatable'?"
"substitution of capital for labor"
쓰는 법: "If automation is the substitution of capital for labor — who owns the capital determines who wins. Is that the core problem?"
"structural unemployment"
쓰는 법: "This isn't just unemployment — it's structural unemployment. The jobs exist, the skills don't match. Is that actually fixable?"
"lender of first resort"
교육 문제 얘기할 때. "The government is the lender of first resort for students."
"socially acceptable behavior" vs "ethical"
쓰는 법: "You draw a distinction between machines being ethical and machines behaving in socially acceptable ways. Who decides what's acceptable?"
"break the chain of responsibility"
전쟁/자율무기 파트. "When you break this chain of responsibility — nobody is responsible."

Quick Reference

인터뷰어
필수 표현

B1-B2 레벨 맞춤형. 입에 붙을 때까지 소리 내어 연습해둬.

상황별 핵심 표현
맞장구
"That's a great point." / "I see what you mean." / "Absolutely."
상대의 논리를 이해했음을 보여줌. 과하지 않게.
질문 연결 — 마법의 구절
"That leads me to ask..." / "Building on that," / "Which brings me to..."
이것만 잘 써도 질문이 툭툭 끊기지 않고 대화처럼 들림. 특히 "That leads me to ask"는 필수 암기.
구체적 사례 요청
"Can you give me an example?" / "What does that look like in real life?"
추상적인 대화를 구체적으로 바꿀 때. 카플란은 사례 들기 좋아하니까 자주 써도 좋아.
쉽게 설명 요청
"In simpler terms..." / "How would you explain this to a student?" / "How would you put that simply?"
시청자를 핑계 삼는 게 프로페셔널해 보여: "I want to make sure our audience understands it perfectly."
이해 확인 / 요약
"So, you're saying that..." / "If I understand correctly..."
내 이해가 맞는지 확인하며 흐름 정리. 카플란이 보정해줄 기회도 줌.
날카로운 반론 제기 (부드럽게)
"Some people might say..." / "Critics would argue that..."
내 의견이 아닌 제3자 의견인 척 반론 제기. 직접 공격하지 않으면서 첨예하게 만들 수 있어.
못 알아들었을 때 — 프로답게
"I'm sorry, could you explain that one more time in simpler terms? I want to make sure our audience understands it perfectly."
내가 못 알아들었다기보다 "시청자를 위해" 뉘앙스. 훨씬 프로페셔널해 보여.
질문이 길어질 것 같을 때
"My next question is..." / "I'd like to ask you about..."
복잡한 연결어 대신 단순하게 선언. 문법 실수도 줄고 전달력도 높아짐.
마무리
"Any final thoughts for our viewers?" / "Is there anything you'd like to add?"
인터뷰를 깔끔하게 닫는 표준 표현.
💡 입에 붙이기 — 3가지 핵심 암기
1. "That leads me to ask..."
가장 중요. 이것만 자연스럽게 나와도 인터뷰 흐름이 완전히 달라짐.
2. "So, you're saying that..."
긴 답변 후에 요약해주면 그도 편하고, 영상 편집할 때도 좋은 컷이 됨.
3. "Some people might say..."
직접 반론하기 어색할 때 제3자를 내세워 날카롭게 찌르는 법.
